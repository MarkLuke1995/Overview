Professional Portfolio

This page is meant to be a showcase of projects I completed throughout my school and professional career. It currently exists solely with projects I completed during my educational career at Ohio State where I completed a degree in Mechanical Engineering. Just below this paragraph, a button to download my resume can be found. Below that, and you will find abbreviated versions of my projects! Clicking on these will take you to a more in-depth description of each of these and each specific section I completed. Downloads for the reports will be available on these in-depth sections. One other note is that this web page itself is one of my projects, exploring web development!

Capstone - Team JetCat

My capstone project was to determine a mathematical model of a mini-turbojet engine, the JetCat. In it, the team researched methods to create this model, designed and built a thrust stand, and ran static experimental tests to emprically determine the model. My main role was in the design and analysis of the thrust stand and manufacturing of it.

CAD Chillout Desktop Fan

The CAD Chillout Desktop Fan was a project with three other team members during my CAD/CAM technical elective. The goal of the project was to reverse engineer a common item with complex geometries within SolidWORKS, and use more complex analysis tool within the program on the model. I was in charge of modelling four structural pieces and performing the drop test.

Machine Elements - Real Mech

The machine elements project was a cumulative assignment from the two machine elements courses I went through. With a team, the goal was to utilize many machine elements and create an kids exhibit for science museum. The elements had to withstand 102 days of usage without maintenance and maintain a safety factor of 1.25.

CAD / CAM Injection Mold Design

Injection Mold Design was one of the 10 labs in the CAD/CAM class that utilized Tormach 770 CNCs. The premise of the project was to design an injection mold using SolidWORKS mold tools and with several parameters, machine it out, and use the injection press to create a plastic mold. I created a NASA logo.

Radiation Detection and Instrumentation Labs

These labs were part of the radiation detection and instrumentation technical elective and took part at Ohio State's research reactor. I was able to use neutron proportional counters, HPGe detectors and other instruments along with single and multichannel analyzers. My nuclear voltaic battery abstract is also showcased in this section.

MagLev Train Controller Design

The MagLev train controller design project was a semester long project for my Systems and Controls course. It touched on every part of controls, from determining equations of motions for a simple MagLev train system, linearizing the EoMs about an operating point, and using MatLabs sisotool to design a PID controller for the system.

Arduino Prototyping Lab

Arduino prototyping lab explored using a microcontroller (in our case, an arduino uno) and several sensors to create a working prototype of a product. I created a door lock sensor that utilized a accelerometer, IR distance senser, and servo motor as well as a 7 clock LED display and RBG LED.

CAD 3D Printed Chess Piece Design

The CAD 3D Printed Chess Piece involved creating a standard chess piece with a twist in SolidWORKS while still pertaining to several design parameters. I created a rook with a rounded top and "cannons" coming out the base. The design was then 3D printed, and a mold was created from the piece.

FEH Robot Project

The FEH robot project was a semester long project during my second semester of college. Our team, the Copperheads, was tasked with creating an autonomous robot to complete a course with several tasks. I was in charge of the fabrication and coding of the robot.

Team JetCat

Team JetCat was my capstone project over the course of the 2018-19 school year. The scope of our project was to research about jet engine models, design a thrust stand, and statically and dynamically test the mini-turbojet engine. The reason for this project was because there was no model to accurately predict the engine performance for a throttle input to thrust output, and this nearly caused an OSU research UAV to crash during the previous year. Our team ended up being able to statically test the engine for a variety of step throttle inputs, but was unable to complete wind tunnel testing. More detail can be seen in the PDF to the right.

My main roles during the project were to help with the design and manufacturing of the thrust stand, and to oversee the testing of the engine. Some of the more notable things I did was the brainstorming of the engine mounting piece to the vertical beam, bolting analysis of the thrust stand, CNC milling of one of the baseplates and bottom engine mounting plate, and addition of a diagonal beam to the thrust stand to increase overall stiffness and reduce vibration during testing. The primary sections of the report I was responsible for included Primary Research (pg. 8), Needs Chart (pg. 11), bolting analysis (pg. 29-31), Thrust Stand Development (pg. 39-40), and Conclusions/Future Work (pg 53).
